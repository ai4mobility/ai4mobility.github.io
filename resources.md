# Resources

A curated list of references, datasets, tools, and refresher links used throughout the course.

## Reference textbooks

**Machine learning and deep learning**

- Géron, A. (2022). *Hands-On Machine Learning with Scikit-Learn, Keras, and TensorFlow* (3rd ed.). The most practical reference for getting things working.
- Goodfellow, Bengio, & Courville (2016). *Deep Learning.* Free online at [deeplearningbook.org](https://www.deeplearningbook.org/).
- Murphy, K. P. (2022). *Probabilistic Machine Learning: An Introduction.* Free online at [probml.github.io](https://probml.github.io/pml-book/).

**Transportation and traffic flow**

- May, A. (1990). *Traffic Flow Fundamentals.* The classic text.
- Treiber, M. & Kesting, A. (2013). *Traffic Flow Dynamics.* Modern, readable.
- Daganzo, C. (1997). *Fundamentals of Transportation and Traffic Operations.* Concise and rigorous.

**Autonomous vehicles and ADAS**

- Pendleton et al. (2017). *Perception, Planning, Control, and Coordination for Autonomous Vehicles.* MDPI Machines (open access).
- Yurtsever et al. (2020). *A Survey of Autonomous Driving: Common Practices and Emerging Technologies.* IEEE Access.

## Open datasets for AI mobility

| Dataset | Use case |
|---|---|
| [KITTI](http://www.cvlibs.net/datasets/kitti/) | Autonomous driving (cameras, lidar, GPS) |
| [Waymo Open Dataset](https://waymo.com/open/) | Large-scale AV perception and prediction |
| [nuScenes](https://www.nuscenes.org/) | Multi-sensor AV dataset |
| [BDD100K](https://bdd-data.berkeley.edu/) | Driving videos for detection, segmentation |
| [Cityscapes](https://www.cityscapes-dataset.com/) | Urban scene segmentation |
| [UA-DETRAC](https://detrac-db.rit.albany.edu/) | Vehicle detection and tracking from traffic cameras |
| [PeMS](https://pems.dot.ca.gov/) | California traffic flow data (loop detectors) |
| [NGSIM](https://ops.fhwa.dot.gov/trafficanalysistools/ngsim.htm) | Vehicle trajectories on US-101 and I-80 |
| [Argoverse 2](https://www.argoverse.org/) | AV motion forecasting |
| [SUMO scenarios](https://sumo.dlr.de/docs/Tutorials/) | Traffic simulation networks |

## Tools and frameworks

- **PyTorch** — deep-learning framework used throughout this course.
- **Ultralytics YOLO** — fastest path to a working object detector ([docs](https://docs.ultralytics.com/)).
- **OpenCV** — classical computer vision.
- **SUMO** — open-source microscopic traffic simulator ([sumo.dlr.de](https://sumo.dlr.de/)).
- **CARLA** — open-source AV simulator ([carla.org](https://carla.org/)).
- **Flow / SUMO-RL** — reinforcement-learning environments for traffic control.
