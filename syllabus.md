# Syllabus

## Course information

**AI Mobility Technologies**  
A graduate-level course on artificial intelligence applications in transportation.

- **Format**: Self-paced, open materials (recordings + notes + labs)
- **Estimated time**: ~40 hours total (~10 hours per module)
- **Prerequisites**: See {doc}`prerequisites`

## Learning objectives

By the end of the course, students will be able to:

1. Describe the major families of AI methods (supervised learning, deep learning, computer vision, reinforcement learning) and identify which mobility problems each is suited to.
2. Implement and train a vehicle-detection model on traffic camera footage.
3. Analyze the role of AI components within ADAS and autonomous driving stacks, and reason about safety / efficiency trade-offs.
4. Use AI-augmented microscopic traffic simulation to evaluate transportation interventions.
5. Critically read AI-mobility research papers and identify methodological strengths and weaknesses.

## Schedule

| Week | Module | Topic | Lab |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1–2 | Module 1 | Foundations of AI for Mobility | Python + ML warmup |
| 3–5 | Module 2 | AI Sensing for Traffic Monitoring | Vehicle detection with YOLO |
| 6–9 | Module 3 | AI for Driving Automation and Connectivity | Lane-keeping with a CNN |
| 10–12 | Module 4 | AI Traffic Simulation and Digital Twin | RL traffic signal control |

*(Exact pacing depends on your background. Self-learners typically take 8–14 weeks.)*
